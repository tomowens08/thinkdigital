Lets begin our explanation with a look at the largest and most dominate area of advertising online, pay per click.

Pay-per-click

Pay per click essentially is the potentially very expensive area of paying for advertising on Google or Bing, mainly Google – by far, in an auction format. Its a Dutch auction like the stock market. So pricing can get out of hand, it is essential that you don't throw caution to the wind and assume that simply throwing money at this problem will work. Sometimes it, will but that depends on the competition in the market place for that industry or specifically, the keyword in question.

Lets begin my examining Google and it AdWords program as a kind of case study, the model is closely followed by competitors.

Google Advertising

Googles system is based on keywords, the phrases that people search for, based on the volume that people search that phrase, bidders will try to secure top page results for this. This position is not exclusively determined by cost per click bid. Any Google training program, such as AdWords fundamentals, will tell you that organic position play a art and of you bigger rivals have secured good natural SEO position, their rank will increase in a geometric fashion based on their bids. What is more, each ad will serve as back link which , if you read up on SEO [ link to SEO page here] will increase the position of their naturally placed search engine entry, meaning leads through that point, the click through rate determined leads, are pushed to the website at no charge.

Much like building a rocket to the moon, its better to let the engine hum and see what results are like for the website, how the user engage with it and what bad links or black lists show up, even undesirable keyword positioning, when the website has the exposure of PPC. This is done and works like a warm up lap where the intention is not to break the bank. Never run a race-car without warming the tyres!

There are other search engines of course, more notable are Bing and Duckduckgo for example. Lets look at Bing now:

Bing and Peripheral search engines.

Bing uses a very similar platform as Google and inclusion is very important. One important consideration is that if your clients are company directors then know that many business uses Microsoft tech stack as standard. Its a testament to the survivalist nature of the company that they have lock down these clients through a mixture of MS server requirement and network installation defaults on Windows.

As a result you may consider some marketing through PPC on Bing especially on trial basis as its much cheaper, the click through rate is much higher and the cost per click lower, therefore to trade through the platform makes more sense even if the volume is much lower. Selling to the general public where volume is necessary may make this process virtually futile.
